Fruits are a perfect way of gaining some major nutrients and are the best source of Vitamin A, vitamin C, and some other minerals.
They are also considered the best sources of dietary fiber. Fresh fruits are generally low in calories because they contain enough water in them with a little fat.
But in the case of frozen foods, the addition of sugar will result in increasing the calories. We generally refer to fruits by their sweet or sour taste,
but fruits are a seed-bearing structure in biological terms. In this article, we will discuss all different types of fruits along with their properties.
After reading the interesting facts about these fruits, you will appreciate them more.

What is a Fruit?

The fruit is just a product of trees that bears the seed and can be consumed by all living beings.
Some of them are sweet, while the others are sour. There are wide varieties of fruits available on the planet. Some of them can be eaten raw,
while some can be eaten in frozen form.
Some of the vegetables, such as tomatoes, cucumbers, squash, etc., are also treated as fruits.

Different Categories of Fruits: Introduction

Fruits are generally divided into some subcategories such as: Achene Achene is a dry fruit developed from a single ovary and consists of a thin wall around its seed. Strawberry, Sunflower Seeds, etc., are some of the examples of Achene. Berry Berries are the most popular types of fruits because of their delicious taste. They are also considered the healthiest food on the planet. Berries are small juicy fruits with a sour, sweet, or tart taste depending on their categories. Strawberries, Blueberries, Raspberry, etc., are some of the examples of Berries. Berries are very healthy because they are packed up with a lot of antioxidants in a small package. Pomes Pomes are those fruits that have a hard covering around their seed. Apples and Pears are examples of Pome fruits. The main characteristic of this category of fruits is its central core, which surrounds the seed. Caryopsis Caryopsis is a type of fruit mostly seen in cereals; it is the single fruit in the walls of the ovaries joined to the outer seed covering. Corn, Rice, Rye, Oats, etc., are some of Caryopsis Fruitsâ€™ most common types. Drupe Drupes are those kinds of fruits with a single seed only and are surrounded by a hard layer. Drupes are generally sweet, juicy fruits, and they are also a rich source of vitamins, minerals, and fibers. Mangoes, apricots, cherries, plums, etc., are different examples of Drupes. Legume Legumes are generally long sized fruits and contain many seeds inside them. These seeds are released when the fruit is peeled along. Peas, Beans, Peanuts, etc., are some different examples of Legumes. Nut A Nut is considered a dry fruit with a tough outer covering, and inside the hard shell, there is a single edible seed. Chestnuts, Hazelnuts, Acorns, etc., are some different examples of Nuts. Citrus Fruits Citrus fruits are also considered as a variety of berries. They come in different colors ranging from yellow to orange. Their segments are filled with tiny liquid-filled carpels that contain citrus juice. The protective covering around the citrus fruits is also very beneficial. It contains volatile oil, which is mostly used in making essential oils. Lemon, Lime, Pomelo, etc., are different examples of Citrus Fruits.
